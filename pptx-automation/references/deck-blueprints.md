# Deck Blueprints

Use these structures as defaults. Adapt them to source material and user intent.

## Company Introduction: IR / Growth Story

Purpose: convince investors or strategic partners that the company has a strong problem, solution, market, traction, and team.

Default flow:

1. Cover: company name, positioning statement, date/version
2. Problem: market/customer pain stated as a clear business issue
3. Solution: product or technology answer
4. Market: target segment and opportunity; use only provided or sourced figures
5. Product/service: key capabilities and use cases
6. Technology moat: proprietary method, data, architecture, patents, or operational advantage
7. Business model: pricing, revenue stream, unit economics if provided
8. Traction: customers, pilots, revenue, retention, usage, awards, partnerships
9. Team: founders, key members, advisors, organization
10. Roadmap/use of funds/next step
11. Closing/contact

Emphasis: growth potential, defensibility, metrics, credibility.

## Company Introduction: B2B Sales

Purpose: establish trust and make the buyer understand why the company can solve their problem.

Default flow:

1. Cover: customer/company-specific title
2. Company overview: who we are, what we provide
3. Customer problem: pain points and business context
4. Solution overview: proposed product/service
5. Core technology or differentiators
6. Use cases and implementation examples
7. Expected impact: efficiency, cost, quality, risk, revenue
8. Customers/partners/reference cases when provided
9. Proposed scope and delivery model
10. Next steps/contact

Emphasis: reliability, clarity, implementation feasibility, customer-specific value.

## Government Task Proposal

Purpose: show necessity, technical feasibility, execution plan, and public/business impact. Align language with evaluation criteria.

Default flow:

1. Cover: project name, organization, consortium if any
2. Task overview: objective, period, participants, expected output
3. Background and necessity: policy, market, technology, field pain
4. Problem definition: measurable gap or limitation
5. Development or demonstration goal
6. Detailed work packages: WP, deliverables, responsible party
7. Technical approach: architecture, method, validation plan
8. Promotion system: organization chart, roles, governance
9. Schedule: phases, milestones, gates
10. Budget/resource plan if provided
11. Expected impact: technical, social, economic, commercialization
12. Utilization and expansion plan
13. Closing summary

Emphasis by subtype:

- Technology development: originality, feasibility, performance metrics, validation.
- Demonstration: field applicability, operation plan, measurable outcomes.
- Commercialization: revenue model, customers, go-to-market, scalability.

## Customer Proposal

Purpose: show the customer's current issue, a concrete solution, delivery scope, timeline, cost, and operating plan.

Default flow:

1. Cover: customer-specific proposal title
2. Executive summary: recommendation and expected effect
3. Customer issue: current pain and constraints
4. Proposed solution: concept and scope
5. Application method: process, integration, architecture, operating model
6. Deliverables: what will be provided
7. Expected impact: business and operational results
8. Schedule: phases and milestones
9. Cost or commercial terms when provided
10. Risk and response plan
11. Next steps/contact

Emphasis: fit to customer, practical implementation, measurable value, clear scope.

## Presentation Readability Cleanup

Use when the source deck already exists and the task is primarily improvement.

Per-slide decision rules:

- If the slide has more than one message, split it or add sectioned hierarchy.
- If the title is only a topic, rewrite it into a conclusion.
- If body text exceeds about 80-100 Korean characters or 45-60 English words, summarize or convert to cards/table.
- If a table has too many columns, group columns, highlight the decision columns, or split into multiple slides.
- If colors or fonts vary without meaning, normalize them to the selected style.
- If a slide has no clear audience action, add a key takeaway or next-step sentence.

Recommended cleanup flow:

1. Cover refresh
2. Agenda or narrative map
3. Section divider only when needed
4. Rebuilt body slides with stronger hierarchy
5. Final summary/contact slide
